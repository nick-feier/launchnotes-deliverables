Most sales orgs find out about new features the same time as customers - or after.

Cvent's VP Enterprise Sales flipped that. He closed a $100K ARR deal mid-cycle by handing the customer the live roadmap on a sales call.

Here's the 4-step play. What he did, the language he used, and what changed on the back end.

Thesis

The product roadmap is a sales asset, not a PM artifact.

Reps who walk into a deal with the same view of "what's shipping next" as the customer close more competitive deals.

The answer to "is X on the roadmap?" lives in the meeting - not in a Slack thread you have to chase down.

Why this works for sales leaders

Sales leaders are graded on revenue.

Forecastio (2025) and SaaStr commentary peg 20-30% of competitive losses to "we already shipped that and didn't know."

For a 35-rep / $60M ARR org running 25% win rate, a 3-point lift on competitive deals is $1.5-2.4M in recoverable ARR per year.

The Cvent play hits both ends: reps see what's shipping at the same moment as customers, and the customer leaves the call with a roadmap they can point at.

Phased rollout

Step 1: Subscribe alongside customers
Week Pre-cycle

Reps see what's shipping the same moment customers do.

No more relying on a forwarded PM email that got buried in someone's inbox.

Activities
  • Reps subscribe to the same published roadmap your customers see
  • Internal release announcements hit the rep's inbox before the customer's inbox
  • Each rep can pull up the live page on any device, any time
Deliverables
  • Every AE on the roadmap distribution list
  • 30-40% internal open rate as the early signal that reps are actually reading it
Gate: Every rep can locate the published roadmap page and answer "what shipped last week" from memory.
Step 2: Bring the roadmap into the deal moment
Week In-call

Don't talk about the roadmap.

Open it.

Activities
  • When the customer asks "is X on the roadmap?" - share screen, pull up the page, walk them through it live
  • Use the published roadmap, not an internal Jira view (the published version is the version the customer trusts)
  • Reference the page by name in follow-up emails so the customer comes back to it themselves
Deliverables
  • Live roadmap shared on screen in at least the next 5 mid-cycle calls
  • Follow-up email links the customer back to the roadmap page
Gate: If a rep can't pull the roadmap up in under 30 seconds in a live call, the page isn't bookmarked. Fix that.
Step 3: Walk through shipped, shipping, and on-deck
Week Mid-call

Specific, named, dated.

The roadmap stops being abstract and starts being a deal conversation.

Activities
  • Three columns: what shipped in the last 60 days, what's shipping this quarter, what's queued next quarter
  • Call out the features that map to the customer's pain - by name, not category
  • Acknowledge what's not on the roadmap. Reps who say "that's not on the plan, but here's what is" build more trust than reps who pretend everything is coming
Deliverables
  • Rep can name 5 shipped features and 3 on-deck features that match the customer's use case
  • Customer leaves the call knowing where on the page to find updates next week
Gate: Customer asks at least one follow-up question about a future feature in the same call. That's the signal the conversation shifted.
Step 4: Close on what's coming next
Week Close

Chad's words: this "completely changed the tone of the conversation."

Customer signed for $100K ARR.

Activities
  • The deal closes on the future state, not the current state
  • Lock in the renewal or expansion conversation around upcoming features (the customer now knows they are real, dated, and visible)
  • Document which roadmap items moved the deal - the same patterns repeat on the next mid-cycle close
Deliverables
  • Closed-won attribution noting which roadmap items influenced the deal
  • A repeatable play that doesn't depend on the rep's product fluency - it depends on the roadmap page existing and being current
Gate: The play replicates. A second deal closes off the same roadmap motion. (Chad ran it again on a $50K life-sciences deal.)
