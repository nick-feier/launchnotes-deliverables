Findings

01 DMARC policy at p=none caps reputation upside

medium Authentication

Your DMARC record is published but enforcement is set to p=none. Mailbox providers downweight senders without enforcement, especially under heavy cold volume.

v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@acme.com
Recommendation

Move to p=quarantine after 2 weeks of clean RUF/RUA reports. Targets a 6-9pp lift in inbox placement at Outlook.

02 Single-IP concentration creates a single point of failure

high Send infrastructure

78% of your outbound volume originates from one IP block. One spam trap hit could throttle the entire campaign.

Recommendation

Split sending across 3-5 mailbox providers or add a secondary IP pool warmed in parallel.

03 Reply rate is low for your spam-trigger profile

low Content

No banned phrases detected in your last 1,000 sends. Reply rate of 1.4% trails category median (2.1%), but the gap is content, not deliverability.

Recommendation

Review opening line variance and CTA framing. Deliverability is not the bottleneck here.

Methodology

Findings derived from public DNS records, MX lookups, and a 30-day send-pattern analysis. No access to your ESP was used.
